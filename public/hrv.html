DIY Heat Recovery Ventilator (HRV) Build Guide

Aluminum plate cross-flow heat recovery ventilator for residential ventilation with energy recovery. Budget-minded design with 60-75% sensible effectiveness.

Overview

Build a 24″ × 24″ × 10–12″ cross-flow plate core from thin aluminum sheets separated by 1/8″ spacer strips to form alternating channels. Aluminum's thermal conductivity (~190–230 W/m·K) is orders of magnitude higher than plastics (~0.16–0.20 W/m·K for polycarbonate), which reduces wall resistance and typically bumps sensible effectiveness into the upper end of the common cross-flow range (60–75%) when pressure drop is kept low.

Key Benefits

  • High thermal conductivity: Aluminum plates conduct ~200× better than polymer plates
  • Cost-effective: Total budget $1,365–$1,625 for complete system
  • Simple construction: Stackable plate design with foam tape spacers
  • Quiet operation: Low face velocities minimize pressure drop and noise

Build Instructions

1. Cabinet Construction

Build a 26″×26″×16″ box from 1″ foil-faced polyiso insulation board. Seal all seams with foil tape.
  • Cut four round ports (10–12″ diameter):
    • OA-IN & SA-OUT on one diagonal
    • RA-IN & EA-OUT on opposite diagonal
  • Install access panel for maintenance
  • Add drain pan provisions at bottom

2. Plate Preparation

From aluminum rolls, shear 90–100 plates to 24″×24″. Square and deburr all edges, then clean with alcohol.
  • 4 rolls yield ~100 plates (12″ stack)
  • 3 rolls yield ~75 plates (9″ stack)
  • Ensure all plates are identical size
  • Remove any protective film

3. Spacer Application

Apply 1/8″ foam tape along two adjacent edges of each plate in alternating pattern.
  • Odd plates: Tape north & west edges
  • Even plates: Tape south & east edges
  • This creates sealed manifolds for crossing airstreams
  • Press tape firmly for good adhesion

4. Core Assembly

Stack plates to target thickness (9–12″). Keep corners perfectly flush throughout assembly.
  • Check alignment every 10 plates
  • Apply light, even pressure while stacking
  • Install aluminum angle frame around perimeter
  • Add threaded rod through-bolts at corners
  • Tighten just enough to compress gaskets

5. Edge Sealing

Seal all four vertical edges with continuous foil tape bands to prevent crossover leakage.
  • Apply foil tape in overlapping layers
  • Run silicone bead at all corners
  • Seal any gaps around frame
  • Allow silicone to cure 24 hours

6. Drainage Setup

Pitch cabinet 1–2% toward exhaust-leaving corner. Install shallow aluminum pan with drain.
  • Condensate forms on cold side in winter
  • Connect drain to appropriate discharge
  • Test drain flow before final installation

Fan & Control Installation

Fan Mounting

  • Mount each ATO EC 280mm fan to sheet-metal transition plenums
  • Transition from fan outlet to 10–12″ round duct
  • Use vibration isolators to minimize noise transmission
  • Ensure proper clearance for fan inlet

Control Wiring

  • Connect both fans' 0–10V control wires to single speed control knob
  • Power control circuit with 24 VDC adapter
  • This ensures balanced supply and exhaust flows
  • Optional: Add separate controls for independent adjustment

Filter Installation

  • Install 20×20×2 MERV-13 filters on both OA and RA intakes
  • Build simple gasketed frames upstream of core
  • Ensure easy access for filter changes
  • Label filter direction and change schedule
Important: Install backdraft dampers in OA intake and EA discharge ducts to prevent reverse flow when system is off.

Commissioning & Operation

Initial Setup

  1. Flow Balancing: Adjust both EC fans with 0–10V control to achieve equal OA and EA flows
  2. Verification: Use pitot tube + manometer or hot-wire anemometer at grilles
  3. Target Flows: 550–650 CFM per stream at moderate fan speed
  4. Pressure Check: Verify slight negative pressure in exhaust stream

Frost Control

If ice forms on core below 20–25°F outdoor temperature:

  • Reduce or temporarily stop OA fan
  • Consider adding timed defrost relay
  • Install preheater for extreme climates
  • Monitor drain for freeze-ups

Performance Expectations

Efficiency Characteristics

  • Sensible Effectiveness: 65–75% typical for well-built aluminum cross-flow cores
  • Pressure Drop: 0.2–0.4 in.w.g. at design flow (including filters)
  • Power Consumption: 100–200W total for both fans at typical speeds
  • Noise Level: <40 dBA at 3 feet when properly installed
Note: Counter-flow aluminum cores can achieve 80–90% effectiveness if you re-stack plates in counter-flow configuration, though this requires different manifolding.
