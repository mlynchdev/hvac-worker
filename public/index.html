Controls & I/O Map

Sensors

  • Room DP sensors (Wood, Fab, CNC) → setpoints: Wood −0.02..−0.05 in. w.g.; Fab slightly negative vs CNC; CNC slight negative.
  • Airflow proving switches on exhaust fans and OA-intake.
  • Current/auxiliary contacts from: dust collector, weld arm fan, grinder hood, CNC mist collector.
  • filter ΔP (all units), fire/smoke damper position.

Actuators

  • VFDs: Wood Louver, Fab/CNC VFD, Fab Exhaust Fan, CNC Exhaust Fan (or one shared exhaust VFD).
  • Motorized OA balancing dampers for Fab & CNC branches.
  • Automatic blast gates on wood dust drops (interlocked with tools).
  • Backdraft dampers on each exhaust branch; fire/smoke dampers on barrier penetrations.

Sequences (summary)

  1. Room pressure tracking: Each space holds its DP setpoint by modulating supply OA (primary) and exhaust VFD (secondary).
  2. Process tracking: When a local source turns on (e.g., weld arm switch closes), its room exhaust VFD ramps to its profile; OA tracks to 90–95% of total exhaust.
  3. Wood dust: Dust collector enables via interlock; auto blast gates open with the tool; wood Louver tracks collector flow to maintain negative pressure.
  4. Safeties: Loss of exhaust → shed OA; damper closure proves before fan start where required; alarm on high filter ΔP.

Field/Design Checklist

  • Confirm simultaneous‑use diversity (Fab: arm + grinder? CNC: number of mist collectors?).
  • Layout duct paths from exterior wall; include long‑radius elbows, cleanouts, test ports.
  • Confirm outdoor placement clearances for cyclone DC, Louver and VFD; verify local code/NFPA compliance.
  • Select silencers/liners as needed (check coolant/mist oil compatibility).
  • Commissioning plan: balance OA/EXH, tune DP setpoints, verify interlocks.
